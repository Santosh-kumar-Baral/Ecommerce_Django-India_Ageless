Experience India's GI, a heritage that's worth a try!

Madhubani painting is a traditional art form from the Mithila region of Bihar, India created by women from various communities which are a major export of Madhubani district.
Araku Valley Arabica Coffee is a specialty coffee grown in the Araku Valley region of India. It is known for its unique flavor profile, which is influenced by the local soil and climate.
Konark Stone Craft is a traditional stone carving craft from the Konark region of Odisha, India. It creates decorative items, statues, and architectural pieces with intricate designs and details through intricate stone carving.
Shahi Litchi of Vihar is a type of litchi fruit grown in the Muzaffarpur district of Bihar, India. It is known for its large size, sweet flavor, and delicate texture.
The Kutch Embroidery is a handicraft and textile signature art tradition of the tribal community of Kutch District in Gujarat and Sindh. It is known for its bright colors, mirrors, beads, and intricate embroidery that embellishes the entire fabric.
The Black Clay Pottery of Nizamabad in Azamgarh district of Uttar Pradesh, India is unique type of clay pottery known for its dark shiny body with engraved silver patterns.
Mysore Mallige is a type of jasmine flower grown in the Mysore district of Karnataka, India. Mysore Mallige is a popular flower in South India and is highly valued for its fragrance and beauty.
Malda Mango is a variety of mango fruit from West Bengal, India. It has a juicy pulp, sweet flavor, and unique aroma. Malda Mango is popular for its taste and often used for desserts and pickles.
Saffron of Kashmir is a highly valued spice grown in the Kashmir valley of India. It is known for its unique flavor, aroma, and vibrant color, making it one of the best varieties in the world.
Odisha Rasgulla is a traditional Indian sweet. It originated in the state of Odisha and is known for its spongy texture. The dish is a popular dessert in India and is now enjoyed worldwide.
Kanchipuram Saree is a traditional Indian saree made in the Kanchipuram region of Tamil Nadu, India. Kanchipuram sarees are popular among brides and are a symbol of South Indian culture.

GI Tag?

Geographical Indication (GI) is an intellectual property right indicating a product's originating from a specific geographical region that has certain qualities, characteristics or reputation that are essentially attributable to its origin. GI tags protects traditional knowledge, cultural heritage, and economic interests of the community.

Learn More...

Origin & History

GI tags originated in Europe in the 20th century. In 2003, India established the GI Registry to protect local communities' cultural heritage, traditional knowledge, and economic interests. Over 436 Indian products have been granted GI tags since the establishment of the GI Registry.

Learn More...
Significance

GI tags play a significant role in safeguarding the traditional knowledge, cultural heritage, and economic interests of communities that create distinctive products. They are also beneficial for the local economies as they aid in tourism, employment generation, and enhancing the reputation of the region.

Learn More...

Benefit

GI tags protect products against unauthorized use, misuse, and infringement of the product's origin and increase market value by creating a unique identity. GI tags can also promote sustainable production practices by encouraging local communities to preserve their traditional knowledge and natural resources.
